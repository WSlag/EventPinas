# EventPH — PROJECT CONTEXT

## App Overview

**App Name:** EventPH  
**Type:** Progressive Web App (PWA)  
**Tech Stack:** React + Vite + Firebase (same architecture as GetGo PH)  
**Developer:** Warlito "Lito" Lagbas — Davao City, Philippines  
**Core Mission:** Mobile-first, affordable event management platform for community and SME organizers outside NCR, with a strong focus on on-site event-day tools that no Philippine competitor currently offers.

---

## Core Differentiator

**On-site event management tools** — the single biggest gap in the entire Philippine event management market:

- Table/seating management with drag-and-drop floor plan builder
- QR code guest check-in at the door
- Walk-in registration on event day
- Real-time attendance dashboard
- Guest coordinator tools for event staff

No current Philippine platform owns this space. HelixPay comes closest with QR entry scanning only, but stops at the door.

---

## Target Market

**Primary:** Community and SME organizers outside NCR — Mindanao (Davao, CDO, Zamboanga), Visayas (Cebu, Iloilo, Bacolod)  
**Secondary:** Schools, barangay councils, civic organizations, church groups, alumni associations  
**Event Types:** Reunions, debuts, birthdays, fiestas, school programs, fundraisers, local concerts, community expos  
**Buyer Profile:** Mobile-first, GCash-native, Taglish-speaking, cost-conscious organizers who currently manage events via Facebook Messenger, Google Sheets, and manual guest lists

---

## Geographic Focus

Davao City and Mindanao region as beachhead market, with expansion to Visayas and provincial Luzon. All major competitors are NCR-centric.

---

# COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE & SWOT ANALYSIS

---

## 1. SM Tickets / Ticketnet / TicketWorld
**Type:** Legacy Distribution Giants  
**Founded:** 1990s–2000s  
**HQ:** Metro Manila  
**Website:** smtickets.com | ticketnet.com.ph | ticketworld.com.ph

### Target Market
- Major concert promoters and live entertainment companies
- K-pop, OPM, and international arena shows
- Sports events, theater productions
- Buyers: Urban consumers in NCR and key cities with credit cards
- Tied to specific venue ecosystems (SM Arena, Araneta, Newport World, Ayala venues)

### SWOT

**Strengths**
- Unmatched national physical reach: SM outlet network spans Butuan, CDO, Davao, Zamboanga, and across all major Philippine cities
- Decades of institutional trust with major live event brands
- Captive venue relationships — major events have no choice but to use them
- Massive mall foot traffic drives spontaneous ticket discovery
- SM Tickets handles high-volume, high-demand events with queue management

**Weaknesses**
- SM Tickets primarily uses physical tickets requiring buyers to claim at outlets with valid ID and the card used for payment — logistical nightmare for provincial buyers
- System crashes and queuing under load during high-demand releases (notorious among K-pop fans)
- Ticketnet does not allow seat selection — the system assigns seats automatically
- Zero tools for small or independent organizers — closed ecosystems for big promoters only
- No organizer analytics, audience data ownership, or brand customization
- Convenience fees add buyer cost without adding digital value
- No GCash-first experience; credit card bias excludes unbanked Filipinos
- No mobile-native or PWA experience
- Zero on-site digital event management tools

**Opportunities**
- Could modernize with a self-serve portal for independent organizers
- Could expand digital ticketing to reduce physical outlet dependency

**Threats**
- Modern SaaS platforms (Ticketnation, HelixPay) eating the independent organizer segment
- GCash-native competitors better suited for younger, unbanked audiences
- Provincial market underserved and increasingly accessible to mobile-first challengers

## 2. Ticketnation
**Type:** Modern SaaS Ticketing Platform  
**Founded:** ~2020  
**HQ:** Makati, NCR  
**Website:** ticketnation.ph

### Target Market
- Independent Filipino event organizers: concert promoters, coaches, fitness instructors, community event hosts, content creators
- Ticket resellers and merchandise sellers
- Digital-native Filipino buyers who prefer GCash and mobile purchases
- Small to mid-scale events (not arena-level)

### SWOT

**Strengths**
- Full GCash, Maya, QRPH, and PayLater integration — most complete local payment stack
- Self-serve SaaS: organizers set up and go live without sales contracts
- Full organizer brand control, audience data ownership, and real-time analytics
- Allows ticket reselling — unique among local platforms
- Expanding into merchandise sales alongside tickets
- Flexible payouts adapted to cash flow needs
- Mobile-first UX designed for Filipino organizers

**Weaknesses**
- Metro Manila-centric in brand visibility and marketing
- Minimal Visayas/Mindanao penetration and no provincial sales presence
- Small event catalogue vs. SM Tickets/Ticketnet limits consumer discovery traffic
- No supplier/vendor marketplace — purely ticketing and commerce
- No venue/table/seating management tools
- No community or social event features (barangay fiestas, reunions, school programs)
- English/urban UX — not localized for Mindanao or provincial Taglish users
- No on-site event management tools (zero day-of features)

**Opportunities**
- Expand to provincial markets which are underserved and GCash-ready
- Add on-site check-in tools to complete the event lifecycle
- Build event discovery marketplace to compete with SM Tickets for consumer traffic

**Threats**
- HelixPay (now Mosaic/PayMongo) with deeper capitalization
- Global platforms like Eventbrite entering PH market
- EventPH targeting its blind spot: provincial organizers + on-site tools

## 3. HelixPay (now Mosaic Solutions)
**Type:** Enterprise Ticketing & Commerce Tech  
**Founded:** 2021 (acquired by Mosaic Solutions, February 2025)  
**HQ:** Makati, NCR  
**Website:** helixpay.ph  
**Funding:** PHP 4B+ annualized transaction value post-acquisition; backed by Foxmont Capital, Gentree Fund, ACTIVE Fund, PayMongo

### Target Market
- Medium-to-large event producers and entertainment companies
- Music festivals, brand activations, premium venues
- Subscription-based businesses, content creators, gym memberships
- Clients: Warner Music Philippines, Anjo World Theme Park, Newport World Resorts, Manila Coffee Festival, Philippine International Hot Air Balloon Fiesta

### SWOT

**Strengths**
- Only Philippine platform with true end-to-end ticketing: online sales → QR on-site scanning
- No-account-required purchase flow improves conversion rates by 50%+ vs. legacy platforms
- No registration fees or subscriptions: charges only ₱20/ticket to buyer
- Custom event websites set up in under an hour with no coding required
- Interactive seat selection maps and e-commerce-style vouchers
- First Philippine subscription/recurring payments platform (GCash, GrabPay, Maya, BDO, BPI, Unionbank)
- Expanded into merch production, on-site payments, and even Starlink internet at events
- Deep capitalization post-Mosaic acquisition with PayMongo integration

**Weaknesses**
- Post-acquisition focus shifting to enterprise F&B/POS clients — ticketing becoming secondary
- On-site tools limited to entry/gate scanning only — stops at the door; no seating, floor plans, table management, or guest coordination
- No event discovery or social marketplace — purely infrastructure/tools
- Manila-headquartered with no Visayas or Mindanao presence
- Pricing and onboarding geared toward events with real budgets; inaccessible to barangay-level organizers
- No table seating, guest list coordination, or social event (debut, reunion) management
- Not self-serve for small organizers — requires engagement with their team

**Opportunities**
- Expand Venue OS into full on-site event management post-Mosaic integration
- Provincial market expansion leveraging PayMongo's nationwide footprint

**Threats**
- Mosaic integration may dilute event-specific focus
- Self-serve competitors (Ticketnation, EventPH) serving the long tail of small organizers
- EventPH specifically addressing the on-site tools gap HelixPay leaves open

## 4. Eventchy
**Type:** AI Event Marketplace (Celebration Segment)  
**Founded:** 2025 (pre-registration August 2025; grand launch December 2025)  
**HQ:** Mandaue City, Cebu (Xugbo Technologies Inc.)  
**Website:** eventchy.com  
**SEC:** 2025030191960-10

### Target Market
- Filipino couples planning weddings
- Families planning debuts, birthdays, and milestone celebrations
- Event suppliers: photographers, caterers, florists, sound systems, venues, stylists
- Primarily Metro Manila and urban centers; beginning to expand nationally

### SWOT

**Strengths**
- First-mover as AI-powered event marketplace in PH celebration space
- AI assistant "Eve" matches clients with suppliers based on budget, location, and style
- Dream Boards feature taps into Filipino social media inspiration behavior
- Verified supplier badges create trust signals in an unregulated space
- No upfront fees for suppliers — commission-only model reduces barrier to entry
- 30 supplier features including booking calendar, in-app chat, digital contracts, AES-256 data encryption, audit logs
- Cebu-based (not Manila) — signals potential provincial awareness
- Featured in Manila Standard, WhenInManila, and other PH media
- RSVP management and e-invitation generator for clients

**Weaknesses**
- No on-site event management tools whatsoever — ends at supplier booking
- No ticketing, QR check-in, seating, floor plans, or event-day coordination
- Still very new — unproven traction, no public user reviews yet
- Supplier verification slow to scale outside Metro Manila
- Narrowly focused on milestone celebrations — no general community, corporate, or ticketed events
- No offline capability or low-connectivity mode for provincial users
- Dependency on app download for full features may hinder adoption in low-tech markets

**Opportunities**
- Expand supplier base to Mindanao/Visayas markets
- Add on-site coordination tools to extend beyond pre-event marketplace
- Corporate events segment currently unaddressed

**Threats**
- EventPH targeting the on-site management gap Eventchy ignores
- Global platforms (WeddingWire, Zola) potentially entering PH market
- Facebook Groups and Messenger remain primary discovery channel for most Filipino event suppliers — user acquisition will be hard

## 5. Xperto
**Type:** Professional Organization Event Management System  
**Founded:** ~2019  
**HQ:** Metro Manila (Wayvent Innovations Inc.)  
**Website:** xperto.ph

### Target Market
- Philippine professional organizations: medical associations, bar associations, chambers of commerce, accounting societies, engineering boards
- Organizations that hold annual conventions, CPD seminars, and credentialing events
- Institutional buyers with recurring event needs and formal procurement

### SWOT

**Strengths**
- Deep all-in-one integration: event management + association membership + credentialing in one system
- Eliminates fragmented tools (Google Sheets, Zoom, bank transfers) for professional orgs
- High client stickiness — once onboarded, switching cost is high
- Recurring revenue model from institutional clients who hold events regularly
- Deep product-market fit for a specific high-value niche

**Weaknesses**
- Extremely narrow segment — professional orgs are a finite and slow-growing market
- No consumer-facing app or public event discovery
- Not self-serve — requires consultation and onboarding; inaccessible to SMEs
- Zero Mindanao/Visayas localization; Manila-centric
- No ticketing marketplace, general public events, or on-site management tools
- No mobile-first consumer product

**Opportunities**
- Expand into government and LGU event management
- Build self-serve tier for smaller professional associations and NGOs

**Threats**
- Global platforms (Glue Up, NordCham-adopted) entering the same niche
- Budget cuts at professional organizations reducing event frequency

## 6. Virnew / Ex-link Events
**Type:** Managed Virtual & Hybrid Event Services  
**Founded:** ~2000 (Ex-link); Virnew ~2020  
**HQ:** Metro Manila  
**Website:** virnew.com | exlinkevents.com

### Target Market
- Corporate clients: quarterly meetings, conventions, product launches (P&G, PayMaya, McDonald's, Sun Life, Metrobank)
- Professional associations and NGOs needing managed virtual events
- Medical and healthcare organizations (The Medical City)
- Events requiring 100–1,000 virtual attendees

### SWOT

**Strengths**
- Built-in polls, e-raffles, quizzes, surveys, word clouds, and live chat — all without third-party apps
- Philippine-friendly payment options: GCash and mobile transfers
- Automated certificate issuance at end of events
- Blue-chip corporate client portfolio builds strong credibility
- Managed-service model means clients don't need to operate the platform themselves
- Ex-link has 25+ years of events industry experience

**Weaknesses**
- Built for the pandemic era — pure virtual demand has significantly shrunk post-COVID
- Managed service = high cost; not affordable for SMEs or independent organizers
- No self-serve product — discovery and onboarding require direct team contact
- No mobile consumer app, no ticketing, no event discovery marketplace
- Attendance cap of 500–1,000 for virtual seminars limits scalability
- No in-person on-site tools — designed purely for virtual/hybrid format
- Business model dependent on corporate budgets which are discretionary

**Opportunities**
- Pivot to hybrid-first tools that serve the now-permanent hybrid event format
- Build a self-serve tier for SME and NGO organizers

**Threats**
- Global virtual platforms (Zoom Events, Hopin, Airmeet) with larger budgets
- Post-COVID normalization reducing demand for virtual-only events
- EventPH and other mobile-first platforms addressing the in-person segment they've vacated

# EVENTPH STRATEGIC POSITIONING

## White Space to Own

1. **On-site event management** — zero Philippine competitors own this
2. **Provincial/Mindanao market** — all competitors are NCR-centric
3. **Community/social events** (reunions, fiestas, debuts, fundraisers) — underserved by all current players
4. **Affordable self-serve for small organizers** — only Ticketnation partially addresses this
5. **GCash-native, mobile-first, offline-capable PWA** — suited for low-connectivity provincial venues

## EventPH's Unique Value Proposition

> "The only Philippine event app that manages your event from invites to check-in — built for organizers outside Metro Manila."

## Competitive Moat to Build

- Be the first to own the "event day experience" layer in PH
- Build a seating/floor plan builder tailored to Filipino event formats (round tables, family-style layouts, debut setups)
- Integrate with GCash deeply for both ticketing and on-site collection
- Target Davao/Mindanao as beachhead — where no competitor has sales or marketing presence
